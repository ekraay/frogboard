# Frog Board — Demo Script

For the Ginza Bazaar / Bon Odori team. A short walkthrough of what works today and where it is headed. Live at **frogboard.vercel.app/ginza-2026**.

---

## The one-liner

Frog Board is a phone-friendly sign-up board for festival help. Volunteers see what is needed, tap, and add their name. No app, no account, no login. The board runs the coordination so you stop chasing people.

A "frog" is a one-off job someone grabs ("hang the banner by 4 PM"). A "shift" is a scheduled slot at a booth.

---

## What it does today (a 2-minute walk-through)

**1. The volunteer view.** Open **frogboard.vercel.app/ginza-2026** on a phone. Tasks are grouped by day, each showing how many helpers are still needed. Tap a lily pad, type your name, you are in. Done.

**2. Share to a patrol or group.** Send a group its own link: **/ginza-2026?group=Scouts**. They see only their tasks and how many are covered. Good for "Hawks, we need three more for Saturday."

**3. Privacy for youth.** Minors show with a first name and last initial ("Tomas R."). Their full name never reaches the public board.

**4. The organizer grid.** At **frogboard.vercel.app/organize** (one shared password) you manage the event like a spreadsheet: type or paste a whole list of tasks, fill a column down, reorder, and "Clear all" with a one-tap undo. Open or close sign-ups with one button.

**5. It remembers.** Every change is logged. A History view shows who did what and when, and you can revert a change (a deleted task comes back with its sign-ups intact).

**6. Pretty, shareable links.** Each event has its own address you can put on a flyer, like **/ginza-2026**. Multiple events can run at once without hiding each other.

---

## Where it is going

**Reminders that do the nagging for you (the big one).** Once someone signs up, the app reminds them before their shift. Text-first, because youth read texts, not email. One message per person even if they took three shifts (never spam). One tap to confirm, or "I can't make it" which reopens the slot and tells the lead. For younger scouts the reminder goes to a parent; older scouts get their own.

**Sort and filter (in progress now).** Sort the organizer grid by any column, and filter the board by day, group, category, or location, stacked together ("Saturday, Scouts, Food").

**Patrol leaders run their own patch.** Appoint a lead for each group and hand them a private link showing their coverage and gaps, so the work delegates the way the Patrol Method intends.

**Across events.** Recognize a volunteer from one event to the next, and answer "who has and has not helped this year?"
